The Entrepreneurial Journey

"This journey began with a question, but it grew into a calling."

Doriane Ihirwe-Teta's entrepreneurial path began with deep research into the struggles of youth-led SMEs under AfCFTA. Using Root Cause Analysis (Fishbone Diagram), Empathy Maps, and Stakeholder Interviews across East Africa, she discovered the invisible hurdles stopping young entrepreneurs from going regional:

📋
Policy Confusion

Confusing and inconsistent cross-border trade policies

💱
Currency Volatility

Unpredictable exchange rates affecting business planning

📶
Infrastructure Gaps

Weak ICT and digital infrastructure challenges

🔍
Limited Awareness

Lack of knowledge about trade rights and opportunities

👥
Representation Gap

Low participation of young women in formal trade spaces

🧠 "Most traders I spoke with didn't even know AfCFTA applied to them."

The Solution

A digital bridge across African borders.

TradeEase is a mobile-responsive, low-data platform designed to help young and women-led SMEs understand, navigate, and capitalize on cross-border trade in East Africa.

Top Features:

🔎
Smart Trade Policy Finder

Shows regulations by country with easy navigation

💱
Live Currency Conversion

Real-time rates for informed pricing decisions

📄
Auto-filled Trade Templates

Export documents simplified and automated

📬
Policy Update Alerts

Stay informed via email or SMS notifications

🎯 Unique Edge:

Unlike complex enterprise tools, TradeEase is built from the ground up for grassroots entrepreneurs — designed to work even in low-bandwidth regions and tailored to local realities.

From Idea to Interface

"I didn't just design a product — I co-designed a solution."

The prototype of TradeEase was tested with youth entrepreneurs from Rwanda, Kenya, and Uganda. Through real-time demos and feedback sessions, Doriane uncovered what mattered most:

🔍 What users loved:
  • Easy interface with no jargon
  • Country-specific trade guides
  • Peace of mind through legal info and templates
🚧 What users asked for:
  • Multilingual versions → being developed
  • Offline access → in planning
  • Visual learning tools → added!
🗣️ "It finally feels like this was built for me. Not some big business with a legal team."
- Feedback from a trader

Reflection

"Youth aren't under-skilled — they're under-acknowledged."

Creating TradeEase brought Doriane face-to-face with hard truths:

Policy vs. Practice

Policy alone is not enough — usability is the bridge to impact

Language Matters

Tech tools fail when they don't speak the users' language

Youth Readiness

Youth are ready — they just need access, confidence, and visibility

🔎 PESTLE Takeaways:

Political

AfCFTA exists but is poorly localized

Economic

Trade costs eat into SME margins

Social

Cultural bias discourages women-led trade

Technological

Infrastructure gaps = opportunity for mobile-first tools

Legal

SMEs fear compliance due to lack of clarity

🧩 Doriane learned: Innovation isn't invention. It's inclusion.
